My Educational Philosophy

I believe that the purpose of education is to provide children with enriching learning opportunities that allow for them to grow as intellectual, social, emotional, and personal beings. It is my priority to create and maintain an inclusive, equitable learning environment for students to thrive in. By nurturing the potential and abilities of each child, while also differentiating instruction accordingly, I aim to ensure that my students feel valued and supported as learners.

As an educator, I believe that student contributions are essential to the functioning of a productive classroom environment. In particular, meaningful learning takes place through active collaboration within the classroom. By designing rich, challenging lessons and learning tasks that promote communication amongst peers, students will have the opportunity to co-construct knowledge, understand diverse perspectives, and combine their unique strengths in purposeful manners.

My ultimate goal as an educator is to not only enhance the knowledge and skills of students, but to also guide them in becoming empowered, productive citizens of society who embrace diversity and lead ethically responsible lives.

Classroom Management

Promoting a Positive Classroom Environment

In order for children to learn effectively, it is vital to establish and maintain a safe, positive classroom environment that supports all individuals. It is important to not only collaboratively design norms and expectations that emphasize inclusivity, kindness, and respect, but it is also essential to consistently model what each concept looks and sounds like on a daily basis. I will utilize positive reinforcement to encourage prosocial behavior and will promote a growth mindset in order to ensure that students understand that through dedication and perseverance, they can continuously develop their abilities.

Establishing Norms and Expectations

I believe that student contributions are both valuable and essential to the functioning of a productive classroom environment. In order to create a classroom for students to thrive in, I will support children as they collaboratively work together to establish norms and expectations of themselves. These agreed upon norms will be designed to maximize learning, while also holding students accountable and responsible for their actions. Norms and expectations will be signed by all students and displayed in a visible area of the classroom for frequent access and reference. By designing norms and expectations at the beginning of the school year, students will have a positive foundation on which to learn and flourish.

Classroom Organization

Upon entering my classroom, one will notice student work displayed throughout. I believe that it is essential for students to feel proud of their contributions, so intentionally decorating the classroom with each child’s work will serve as a reminder of progress as well as provide motivation to continue growing as collective and individual learners.

In order to promote and facilitate meaningful collaboration in the classroom, I will organize desks into groups consisting of 4-6 students. This will allow for each child to consistently have an “elbow-partner(s)” to share thoughts with as well as a table group to exchange ideas and perspectives with. Further, I will support the diverse learners within my classroom by providing flexible seating options. Standing desks, wobble stools, balance balls, floor seating, as well as traditional desks and chairs will give students choice as they individually select where they would like to position themselves in order optimize learning. To continue, I will have a teacher table situated along one of the walls in the classroom that will allow for me to provide small-group, focused instruction while maintaining visibility of the class as a whole.

Informal Assessment Strategies

Partner and Whole-Class Discussions

I believe that collaboration is an integral part of learning. In order to facilitate this process, I ask open-ended questions and provide students with ample opportunities to think-pair-share. The combination of partner and whole-class discussions permits me to observe as well as listen to students as they communicate with one another. As I note student responses, I gain insight into levels of understanding with regard to the topic, which informs my instruction moving forward.

Using Whiteboards During Instruction

I frequently have students utilize whiteboards during mathematics instruction in order to gain an understanding of how well the children are grasping the topic of focus. The strategy allows for me address misconceptions and adjust instruction in order to meet the needs of each of my students.

Data Driven Instruction

As an educator, I believe it is important to continuously analyze student work and progress as well as reflect upon my teaching practices. This process of evaluation permits me to identify areas of strength and areas of growth. As I gather and analyze data, I am able to make meaningful instructional decisions that allow for me to meet the collective and individual learning needs of my students. 

In order to monitor student progress toward identified standards, I utilize assessment charts. Each column specifically focuses on a key part of the standard, so I am able to understand where students have succeeded as well as where students have struggled. 

According to the data on the chart, I learned that students consistently struggled with explaining how the main idea of the informational text was supported by key details. I designed a whole-class re-engagement lesson that specifically focused on the skill, and individually met with my struggling students in order to provide them targeted guidance.
